UX Design Internship at a Startup

day100 is an early-stage startup with a vision to reinvent recruitment. Its platform helps employers screen candidates through trustworthy references. day100 collects insightful information from people that used to work with the candidates and let employers know about their candidates better already even before they get to the interview stage.

Overview

I worked closely with one of the co-founders to design the experience of candidate users and referrer users for the first release version.

My role
  • UI design
  • UI Development
  • Email Design & Implementation
Additional links
  • day100 website

Design Process

I cannot share hi-fi designs or the final product due to the DNA.

Create Personas

Learnt about potential job candidates and potential referrers. Created personas to identify their behaviors and needs.

Clarify Requirements

Communicated the personas to the co-founder. Gathered business requirements and understood design constraints. Negotiated the requriements to advocate for user's needs. e.g. Giving candidates enough progress updates so that they can act promptly while ensuring anonymity and genuinity of the reference sources.

Page flows

Backed up by the personas, convinced the co-founder that candidates and referrers should not be treated as two separate user groups. Drew ux flow to sort out the overall information architecture.

Wireframes & Mockups

Wireframed on Sketch. Created mockups with existing UI components. Talked to the graphic designer about new UI components introduced in the candidate/referrer module.

Spec & Implement

Helped implement the user interface. Worked with Bootstrap and SASS. Meanwhile, came up with the system map to spec the system for future reference.

Email Design & Implement

I also helped design the templates for email notifications and newsletters, and implemented them with ZURB's Foundation framework.
