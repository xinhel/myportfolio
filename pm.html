Project Management Tool for Developers

Actually is a web application that helps software development team track their development progress. Targeted to small teams, the application keeps track of Github data and allows better planning.

Overview

I teamed with five developers and one designer to create a web application for the Small Data Lab at Cornell Tech.

Iterative Development

Considering that we didn't have a set of specific requirements to start with, we adopted the interative development model. I planned ahead with developers on the main focus of each iteration.

Determine useful information

Tested whether percentage is a good indication of progress from user's point of view. Found out what additional information could be useful for users to track their progress.

Organize the interface

Tested different ways to visualize the information. Sorted out the hierachy of information and organized the interface based on the hierachy.

Refine the UI and visualizations

Focused on usability issues. Improved visual elements such as the look of buttons, font-size of graph legends, etc..

For Whom We Design?

Small teams with around 5 developers

Challenge

There are various industry practices for software development, which means there would be too many scenarios and use cases for us to work on. Given our time budget and our limited knowledges of industry practices, we had to adapt our scope for feasibility.

Strategy

To better define the scope, I suggested to draw concept maps together to share our understandings. We figured out small development team makes a managable target user group for us.

Rationale

Large development teams are usually structured into sub-teams for better productivity. In spite of the development methodologies being used, it's easier for smaller teams to evaluate completion and track progress of thier work.

Data-driven Interface

To avoid overwhelming the user with a smaterring of visualizations, we organized the page based on hierachy of information.

Top A general view of the development progress of each member, sided by a highlight of tasks due recently. Followed up are supporting contents, which are more detailed and informative.

Middle Progress of ongoing tasks and a list of all tasks.

Bottom A timeline of commits that shows detailed information of issues/difficulties encountered by team members.

Color the information.

Color label the progress. Color can intuitively show how a project is going. Green means everything is on track; Yellow is reminder of slow progress; Red gives warning that the actual progress is falling too much behind.

Highlight delayed tasks. Among the upcoming dues, tasks which are falling behind deadlines are highlited in red, make it easy to identify where the problem/difficulty is comimg from.
