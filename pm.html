Project Management Tool for Developers

Actually is a web application that helps software development team track their development progress. Targeted to small teams, the application keeps track of Github data and allows better planning.

Overview

I worked in team with five developers and two designers to create a web application MVP for the Small Data Lab at Cornell Tech.

For Whom We Design?

Small teams with around 5 developers

Challenge

There are various industry practices for software development, which means there would be too many scenarios and use cases for us to work on. Given our time budget and our limited knowledges of industry practices, we had to adapt our scope for feasibility.

Strategy

To better define the scope, I suggested to draw a concept map together to organize our understandings and locate our target users.

Rationale

Large development teams are usually structured into sub-teams for better productivity. As sub-teams get smaller, they tend to behave more similarly in spite of different development methodologies being used, and also embrace less ambibuity. Therefore, small teams make a managable target user group for us.

Color the information.

Color label the progress. Color can intuitively show how a project is going. Green means everything is on track; Yellow is reminder of slow progress; Red gives warning that the actual progress is falling too much behind.

Highlight delayed tasks. Among the upcoming dues, tasks which are falling behind deadlines are highlited in red, make it easy to identify where the problem/difficulty is comimg from.

We Did Concept Mapping

To help define our product.

Before we dived into the design and development, we used concept mapping to identify target users. Drawing the concept map together makes it easy for us share our knowledges with each other. As the main idea branched out, we became more clear about our scope.

To keep everyone on the same page.

We kept the concept map updated along the product development. It served as a helpful documentation that everyone could refer to when get lost.
