Expedia Travel Package Checkout

Expedia's website features a fully responsive design, including its checkout page. Our redesign, focusing on travel package checkout, is optimized for all screen sizes to create a streamlined checkout experience.

Overview

I worked with two other UX designers as a small team, redesigning Expedia's web checkout experience. We worked directly with Expedia's UX team at Bellevue, WA.

Responsive.

one-page design | fake two-page design

Responsive design is not only preferred for Search engine optimization, but also is easier to manage and adapts well to future devices. Having a responsive homepage, Expedia felt the urge to unify the whole shopping experience across different devices.

Mobile first strategy. We started from mobile screen and scaling it up to tablet and desktop.

User Validated.

We conducted usability test in lab with 6 participants.

The overall experience was smooth. Every participant felt confident in what information they need to fill out. None of them skipped any required fields.

There were also minor issues that imply room for improvement. e.g. Wrong names were entered with iOS autocorrect; Small input fields for birthday caused struggles to tap on.
